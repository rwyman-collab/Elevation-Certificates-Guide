Overview & Formula

Must be at minimum base elevation of home OR higher per jurisdiction freeboard requirements
Elevation Formula:
BFE + Freeboard - Lowest Adjacent Grade = Minimum height above ground

Example: BFE 71.0' + 2' freeboard - 68.4' grade = 4.6' (round up to 5.0')

Note: Always round up to the closest foot to add cushion

Glossary & NFIP Quick Reference

Essential terms and federal requirements

BFE (Base Flood Elevation)
The computed elevation to which floodwater is anticipated to rise during the base flood (1% annual chance flood).
Freeboard
Additional height above the BFE (typically 1-2 feet) required by local ordinances for added safety and potential insurance benefits.
EC (Elevation Certificate)
A FEMA form documenting a structure's elevation relative to the BFE, required for NFIP compliance.
SFHA (Special Flood Hazard Area)
Areas subject to inundation by the base flood (Zones A, AE, AH, AO, AR, A99, V, VE).
NFIP
National Flood Insurance Program - Federal program providing flood insurance and regulating development in floodplains.
